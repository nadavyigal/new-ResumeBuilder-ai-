Resumely — Pricing Model Shape

The choice here determines what's free, what's paid, and who actually converts. Pick the shape that fits how people use the app.

A

Freemium — Free preview, pay to export

Anyone can upload a resume, paste a job description, and see the tailored result. Exporting the PDF or copying the full text requires a paid unlock.


Works because

  • User sees value before paying — highest activation
  • Fast first session (no card required)
  • Free tier drives ASO reviews + word of mouth
  • Industry pattern for resume tools (Resume.io, Zety)

Risks

  • Screenshot workaround kills conversion
  • Hard to upsell beyond one export
  • Low LTV if user leaves after one job
B

Credits — Pay per optimization

Buy a pack of credits (e.g. 3 for $4.99, 10 for $12.99). Each job tailoring or expert report costs 1 credit. Top up when you run out.


Works because

  • Matches how job seekers think — per-job cost
  • Low barrier to first purchase
  • No subscription guilt when job search ends
  • Can offer 1 free credit to prove value

Risks

  • Lower predictable revenue than subscription
  • Complex IAP setup (multiple SKUs)
  • Credit anxiety kills activation mid-session
C

Subscription — Monthly / yearly unlimited

Free first optimization to prove value. Then $X/month or $Y/year for unlimited tailoring, all expert features, all templates, and PDF export.


Works because

  • Predictable MRR — best for forecasting
  • Annual plan = higher LTV per user
  • Unlocks all features cleanly — no usage anxiety
  • Works well IF users return for multiple applications

Risks

  • High churn — users cancel once hired
  • Hard sell for a seasonal, episodic use case
  • Requires strong retention hook to justify recurring cost

Context from GTM Plan V0

The existing plan recommends Option A as the fastest-to-test experiment ("free score → paid export"), and flags the episodic-use / low-LTV risk for Option C. But this is your decision.
