Resumely — Proposed Pricing Structure

Built from your model (1 export / 5-pack / 10-pack / unlimited) + market research from WP-2. Prices marked ⚠️ need validation against real AI costs. Click any tier to flag a concern.

Free tier (activation, confirmed by market): Upload resume → 1 full AI optimization → see improved version → 1 PDF export. This is what Teal and Rezi both give free. Gating before this moment is the market anti-pattern.

Tier 1

Single Export

$3.99 ⚠️
one-time IAP
  • Export this tailored resume
  • PDF download + copy text
  • Lowest barrier to first purchase
Market ref: no direct match (Teal gives 1 free). Priced below coffee to reduce friction.

Tier 2 — POPULAR

5-Export Pack

$12.99 ⚠️
$2.60 per export · one-time IAP
  • 5 exports for 5 different jobs
  • Credits don't expire
  • Covers a typical job search sprint
Sweet spot: active job seeker applies to 5–15 roles. Likely highest-volume tier.

Tier 3

10-Export Pack

  • 10 exports — serious job search
  • Credits don't expire
  • Best per-export value
Power user tier. Competes with Rezi's lifetime ($149) as a lower-risk alternative.

Tier 4 — Ultimate

Unlimited Access

$9.99/mo ⚠️
or $49.99/year · subscription

Market: Teal $29/mo, Rezi $29/mo, Kickresume $8/mo annual. Annual-first.

⚠️ What needs CFO validation before these numbers are final

AI cost per optimization
Need: OpenAI billing — actual cost per optimize call (estimate: $0.30–0.80 per full session). This sets the floor on Tier 1 pricing.
Minimum viable margin
Apple takes 30% on IAP (15% if < $1M/year revenue). After Apple cut: Tier 1 nets ~$2.79 before AI cost. If AI cost > $2.79, Tier 1 loses money per transaction.
Hosting + Supabase + Vercel cost per user
Need: provider billing. Low per-user but scales with usage.
Weekly tier consideration
WP-2 research flagged $9-13/week as high-urgency option (Teal does this). Could replace or complement the credit-pack model.
Existing spec conflict to resolve: WP-2-monetization-spec.md was drafted with subscription-first (weekly/monthly/annual), not credit-packs. Your model diverges. The two can coexist — credit packs for transactional users, subscription for power users — but the feature matrix in that spec needs updating.
