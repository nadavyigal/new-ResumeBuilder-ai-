# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-02-14*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Resumely helps job seekers optimize resumes for ATS screening and recruiter readability before they apply.

**What it does:**  
Resumely analyzes a resume against a real job description, surfaces high-impact gaps, and guides users through role-specific improvements. It supports free ATS checks as top-of-funnel and a full optimization workflow after signup.

**Product category:** ATS resume checker / AI resume optimization.

**Product type:** B2C SaaS.

**Business model:** Freemium with Premium subscription for unlimited optimizations and advanced template/control features.

## Target Audience
**Primary audience:** Active job seekers applying to multiple roles (entry-level through mid/senior professionals).

**Secondary audience:** Career switchers and international applicants adapting resumes to ATS-heavy hiring pipelines.

**Primary use case:** Improve resume-to-job matching and raise interview conversion.

**Jobs to be done:**
- Understand why a resume is being filtered or ignored.
- Adapt resume content quickly for each role without rewriting from scratch.
- Ship ATS-safe, recruiter-readable resumes with confidence.

## Problems & Pain Points
- Applicants submit many resumes but hear nothing back.
- Resume quality is hard to diagnose objectively.
- Job-specific tailoring is slow and mentally expensive.
- Users fear ATS rejection from formatting/content mistakes.
- Existing tools feel generic, opaque, or overpromise outcomes.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct competitors:** Resume optimization tools with ATS scoring.  
**Secondary competitors:** Resume builders and template libraries.  
**Indirect competitors:** Manual editing, career coaches, generic AI chat tools.

## Differentiation
- Free, low-friction ATS check at the top of funnel.
- Role-specific recommendations tied to real job descriptions.
- Privacy-first handling approach in free flow.
- Clear progression from diagnosis to full optimization.
- Bilingual EN/HE experience for local market coverage.

## Objections & Responses
| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "AI outputs can be generic or wrong." | Position as guided drafts; user controls final edits and validation. |
| "Will this guarantee interviews?" | No guarantee claims; focus on better alignment and clearer signaling. |
| "I do not trust resume tools with my data." | Emphasize privacy-first handling and transparent data practices. |
| "I can do this manually." | Highlight speed, prioritization, and repeatability across many applications. |

## Customer Language
**Common phrases to reflect:**
- "I keep applying and hearing nothing."
- "My resume disappears into ATS."
- "I need to tailor quickly for each job."
- "I do not know what to fix first."

**Words to avoid:** Guaranteed outcomes, exaggerated percentages without evidence, vague buzzwords.

## Brand Voice
- Clear, practical, and direct.
- Trust-first and non-hype.
- Helpful expert tone with transparent limits.

## Proof Framework
- Product behavior proof (what the system actually does).
- Process clarity proof (how guidance is generated and applied).
- Evidence-only quantitative proof (published only when validated in analytics docs).
